Cleansers & Serums

Cleansers

Cleansers are a vital part of your routine to maintain healthy, clean skin. However, choosing a cleanser does not need to be complicated. To keep things simple, I have listed a few cleansers categorized by skin type. Skin type can be determined by washing your face with water and determining how your skin feels a short time after. If your skin feels tight, then you most likely have dry skin. Oily skin is somewhat obvious as your skin will feel somewhat oily. Lastly, there is also a category for combination skin for those who may have different areas of the face experiencing both oily and dry skin.

    Dry Skin
    • Neutrogena Ultra Gentle Hydrating Cleanser
    • Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser
    • CeraVe Hydrating Facial Cleanser

    Oily Skin
    • First Aid Beauty Skin Rescue Deep Cleanser
    • CeraVe Foaming Facial Cleanser
    • Neutrogena Oil-Free Acne Wash

    Combination Skin
    • Kora Organics Foaming Cleanser
    • Cetaphil PRO Foaming Face Wash
    • Philosophy Purity Made Simple Facial

Serums

Serums are a fun addition to a skincare routine that are not necesarily required for healthy skin, but definitely have been scientifically proven to improve the look and feel of skin. Below are three types of serums that can be a possible addition to a routine. However, it is important to mention that some serums do not mix well, so best practice for beginners is to start with one serum, and do research before making additions.

    AHA/BHAs

    Best for removing black heads and stubborn build up, these acids work to chemically break down any impurities accumulated throughout the day.

    • SkinMedica AHA/BHA Cream
    • SkinCeuticals Glycolic Renewal Cleanser
    • The Ordinary AHA 30% + BHA 2% Peeling Solution

    Vitamin C

    Best for those will dull skin facing some harsh conditions throughout the day. Vitamin C is a brightening serum that will bring a hydrating glowly look.

    • SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic
    • Drunk Elephant C-Firma Fresh Day Serum
    • Vichy LiftActiv Brightening Skin Corrector

    Retinol

    Best for those wanting to combat stubborn acne or wrinkles, retinol is a more advanced serum that can cause irritation, especially if used incorrectly. For retinol, start with a low percentage and no more than a pea-sized amount for the entire face. However, I suggest using retinol solely on targeted areas.

    • RoC Retinol Correxion Anti-Aging Sensitive Skin Night Cream
    • The Inkey List Bakuchiol Retinol Alternative Moisturizer
    • Versed Press Restart Gentle Retinol Serum
